## Why

This isn't bar/pub trivia — it's the annual friends' beach trip, and the app
should sound like it. The current copy and design language lean on bar/pub
framing that doesn't fit. We also want the game to feel personal with a few very
light inside jokes, used sparingly so they land instead of getting old.

## What Changes

- Remove all **"bar" / "pub" / "pub quiz"** language from user-facing copy and
  replace it with **friends' beach-trip** framing.
- Weave in **subtle, occasional inside-joke allusions**, only where the spot is
  naturally right — never forced, never frequent. Known starters:
  - Anthony's romantic exploits with older women
  - Jonathan wearing khakis on the beach
- Treat the inside-joke list as **extensible** (more may be added later) and keep
  the bar low: when in doubt, leave it out.

Open questions for the research phase (intentionally not decided yet):
- Where (if anywhere) the inside jokes actually fit cleanly — empty states,
  loading lines, round transitions, the landing page tagline?
- Whether to centralize copy so jokes/wording are easy to add later.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities
- `branding-voice`: the product's user-facing lexicon and tone — beach-trip
  framing, no bar/pub language, and the sparing inside-joke policy.

## Impact

- All user-facing copy across the four screens (contribute, host, play, display),
  page titles, and the README's product-facing description. Copy-only; no
  behavior change.
